## How to Find a Trustworthy Web Designer

Handing your brand, your budget, and your online presence to someone you've never worked with before requires real trust. And trust isn't something you should extend automatically — it should be earned through evidence.

Here's how to evaluate trustworthiness before you commit to anyone.

### Trust Signal #1: They Have a Verifiable Online Presence

A trustworthy web designer or agency will have:

- A professional website (that actually works well — slow, broken sites are disqualifying)
- A Google Business Profile with real reviews
- A LinkedIn presence with verifiable history
- Consistent branding and contact information across channels

If the only place you can find them is a Facebook post or a link someone sent you with no other traceable information, that's not enough.

### Trust Signal #2: Their Portfolio Links to Live, Functioning Sites

Screenshots prove nothing. Anyone can screenshot someone else's work. A designer you can trust will link you to live websites you can click through, test on your phone, and verify are real.

If they hesitate to provide live links or claim the sites are "down for maintenance," that's a flag.

### Trust Signal #3: They Can Provide References

Past clients who are willing to speak about their experience are the strongest trust signal available. Ask for one or two references and actually contact them. Ask:

- Did the designer deliver what was promised?
- Were there any surprises in pricing or timeline?
- How was communication throughout the project?
- Would you hire them again?

A designer who can't — or won't — provide references has something to explain.

### Trust Signal #4: They Use a Written Contract

A contract protects both parties. A designer who uses a formal, written project agreement is operating professionally — they're not just hoping things go well, they're structuring the engagement so both sides are clear and accountable.

If a designer resists putting things in writing, walk away.

### Trust Signal #5: Their Communication Is Consistent and Clear

From your very first inquiry, pay attention to how a designer communicates. Do they:

- Reply within a reasonable time (24–48 hours for an initial inquiry)?
- Write clearly and professionally?
- Ask intelligent questions about your project?
- Follow up when they say they will?

Early communication is your best available data on how they'll behave once the project is underway and you've handed over a deposit.

### Trust Signal #6: Their Pricing Is Transparent

Trustworthy designers can explain their pricing clearly. They can tell you what's included, what's extra, and what commonly causes prices to change. They don't hide behind "it depends" without further explanation.

Vague pricing is often a setup for surprise charges. Clear pricing is a sign of professional operations.

### Trust Signal #7: They Set Realistic Expectations

A designer you can trust won't promise what they can't deliver. They won't guarantee Google rankings. They won't promise a complete custom build in 24 hours. They won't tell you your project is easy when it isn't.

Realistic expectations upfront are far more valuable than inflated promises that won't be kept.

### Trust Signal #8: They Have Longevity

How long have they been in business? A designer or agency that has been operating for several years has survived enough client relationships — good and complicated — to know how to handle projects professionally. Longevity isn't a guarantee of quality, but it's a meaningful signal.

### When Your Gut Says No

Trust your instincts. If something feels off — if communication is evasive, if the prices seem designed to obscure rather than clarify, if you feel rushed or pressured — listen to that feeling. The right designer for your business should leave you feeling informed, respected, and confident.

If that's not happening in the conversation before money changes hands, it won't magically improve after.
