## How Much Does a Website Cost From a Professional Designer?

This is one of the most searched questions in the web design industry — and one of the most inconsistently answered. Prices quoted online range from $200 to $50,000 for what's described, vaguely, as "a professional website."

Here's a detailed, honest breakdown of what professional website design actually costs in Canada, and what drives the price.

### What "Professional" Actually Means

Before getting into numbers, it's worth defining the term. A professional website is not just a functioning website. It is:

- Designed with your specific customers and conversion goals in mind
- Mobile-responsive and fast-loading across all devices
- Built with clean SEO foundations (proper structure, metadata, page speed)
- Accessible and functional — every form, link, and feature works
- Owned by you — not locked in a proprietary system you can't transfer
- Supported after launch

A $200 Fiverr site can technically go live and display information. It is not a professional website by this definition.

### Professional Website Pricing by Project Type

**Simple Small Business Site (5–8 pages)**  
What's included: Home, About, Services, Contact, basic interior pages. Mobile-responsive, SEO-ready, contact form, analytics setup.  
**Typical cost: $997–$2,500**  
Best for: Service businesses, trades, consultants, local retailers, professional services.

**E-commerce Website (up to 50 products)**  
What's included: Everything above, plus product pages, shopping cart, payment processing integration, basic inventory management.  
**Typical cost: $2,000–$6,000**  
Best for: Small online retailers, product-based businesses launching direct-to-consumer.

**Booking/Appointment-Focused Site**  
What's included: Standard small business site plus a booking system (scheduling, reminders, calendar integration).  
**Typical cost: $1,500–$4,000**  
Best for: Clinics, salons, fitness studios, service businesses with appointment-based models.

**Custom Web Application**  
What's included: Custom functionality beyond standard websites — member portals, custom calculators, database integration, complex user flows.  
**Typical cost: $5,000–$30,000+**  
Best for: SaaS, platforms, businesses with genuinely unique functional requirements.

### What Pushes the Price Up

- Adding pages beyond the base scope
- Custom photography or original illustration
- Copywriting (content creation for each page)
- Logo design (if not already existing)
- Multilingual support
- Third-party integrations (CRM, booking, payments, live chat)
- Animated or interactive elements
- Ongoing SEO campaign (beyond basic on-page setup)

### What You're Paying for Beyond the Build

A professional designer's price includes more than the hours spent building. You're also paying for:

- Strategy and business thinking that shapes the site
- Years of experience avoiding the mistakes that cost you money
- Process and project management that keeps things on time
- Post-launch support and accountability

This is why a $997 professional build is better value than a $200 non-professional build — not just because the output looks better, but because everything that surrounds the build protects your investment.

### The Ongoing Costs to Factor In

Beyond the build:
- **Domain name:** $15–$30/year (yourcompany.ca or yourcompany.com)
- **Hosting:** $10–$30/month
- **Professional email:** $6–$15/user/month
- **Maintenance:** $75–$200/month if you use an agency plan
- **Future updates:** Hourly or as part of a retainer

A realistic first-year total for a small business website: **$1,500–$4,000** including the build and all running costs.
