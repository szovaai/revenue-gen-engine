## Can I Hire a Web Designer for Just a Landing Page?

Yes — and more often than you'd think, a single well-designed landing page is exactly what a business needs. Not every situation calls for a full multi-page website. Here's when a landing page makes sense, what to expect, and how to hire for it properly.

### What Is a Landing Page?

A landing page is a single-purpose web page designed to achieve one specific goal. That goal might be:

- Capturing email signups for a launch or offer
- Driving calls or bookings for a service business
- Selling a single product or limited-run item
- Promoting an event
- Testing a business idea before building something larger

Unlike a full website, a landing page doesn't have navigation to other pages. It has one message, one call to action, and one conversion goal. Done well, that focus makes it extremely effective.

### When a Landing Page Is the Right Move

**You're testing a new offer.** Before investing in a full website build, a landing page lets you validate that people are actually interested in what you're selling. Run ads to it. See if people sign up or buy. Then invest in the full site once you've confirmed there's demand.

**You're running a campaign.** Sending paid ad traffic to your homepage is usually a mistake — your homepage tries to serve everyone. A dedicated landing page designed around one offer converts significantly better.

**You're launching something new.** A product launch, a new service, a local event — a dedicated page with a countdown timer, clear benefits, and a single CTA is often more effective than pointing people to a general page.

**Your full site isn't built yet.** A landing page can serve as a temporary online presence while the full site is in development. Capture emails, take bookings, make sales — even before your main site is live.

### What Goes Into a Good Landing Page

A professional landing page is not just a simplified website. It's a carefully structured persuasion tool. A great landing page designer will think about:

- **Headline:** The first thing visitors read. It must communicate your value proposition immediately.
- **Subheadline:** Supports the headline and adds detail.
- **Social proof:** Testimonials, client logos, review counts — whatever builds credibility quickly.
- **Benefits over features:** Not "we use proprietary technology" but "you get results in half the time."
- **A clear, prominent CTA:** One action, obvious placement, no confusion about what to do next.
- **Mobile performance:** Many landing page visitors come from ads on mobile. The page must load fast and work flawlessly on phone.
- **Trust signals:** Privacy policy link, SSL, money-back guarantee if applicable, contact info.

### What It Costs

A professionally designed landing page from a boutique agency typically runs **$300–$800** depending on complexity, copy requirements, and whether it includes additional elements like a payment integration or email capture sequence.

Some agencies offer landing pages as a starter project — a good way to test the relationship before committing to a full build.

### What to Ask When Hiring for a Landing Page

- Does the price include copy, or do I provide content?
- How many revisions are included?
- Is it mobile-optimized and tested across devices?
- Who hosts it — me, or you?
- Can it be integrated with my email marketing tool?
- Can this page become part of a larger site later?
