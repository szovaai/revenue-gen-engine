## The Real Price Ranges (Canada, 2024)

Web design pricing in Canada generally falls into four tiers:

**DIY Website Builders (Wix, Squarespace, Shopify): $20–$50/month.** You do all the work. It looks decent if you have a design eye. Most small business owners don't have time to do this well.

**Freelancers (Fiverr, Upwork, local): $500–$3,500.** Results vary wildly. Some freelancers are exceptional; others disappear mid-project or deliver something that looks like 2009 called and wants its website back.

**Boutique Agencies (like ClickAdMedia): $997–$5,000.** You get a done-for-you build, professional strategy, brand alignment, and someone accountable if something goes wrong. This is where most small businesses find the best value-to-quality ratio.

**Large Digital Agencies: $5,000–$50,000+.** Enterprise builds with large teams, long timelines, and pricing to match. Overkill for most small businesses.

## What Drives the Price Up or Down?

Several factors affect your final cost:

- **Number of pages.** A 5-page site costs far less than a 20-page one.
- **Custom design vs. template.** Custom work takes more time and costs more, but the result is unique to your brand.
- **E-commerce features.** Product pages, payment processing, and inventory management all add complexity.
- **Copywriting.** If your designer writes the words for you, expect to pay more. If you provide the content, you save.
- **SEO setup.** Basic on-page SEO, keyword research, and metadata optimization are sometimes included — and sometimes not.
- **Ongoing maintenance.** Some agencies bundle monthly care plans. Others hand you the site and wish you luck.

## The Danger of Going Too Cheap

A $200 Fiverr website sounds appealing until it breaks a week after launch, ranks nowhere in Google, and makes your business look like an afterthought. Cheap websites cost more in the long run — in lost customers, emergency fixes, and eventually rebuilding the whole thing properly.

A legitimate web designer charges what they do because they're protecting your business's online reputation. Your website is often the first thing a potential customer sees. It's your digital storefront. Would you build a physical location out of cardboard to save money?

## Questions to Ask Before Committing

Before signing anything, ask:

- What's included in the price?
- Who owns the website after it's built?
- Is hosting included or separate?
- What does the revision process look like?
- What happens after launch — do you offer support?

If a designer can't answer those questions clearly and confidently, that's your sign to keep looking.
