## Can I Hire a Web Designer for Ongoing Website Maintenance?

Yes — and for many small businesses, ongoing maintenance is one of the smartest investments they make after launch. Your website isn't a set-it-and-forget-it asset. It needs regular attention to stay secure, fast, and effective. Here's what website maintenance typically includes, what it costs, and how to decide if it's right for you.

### Why Websites Need Ongoing Maintenance

A website is more like a car than a building. Leave a car untouched for a year and it'll probably start. Leave a website untouched for a year and you may come back to broken plugins, an expired SSL certificate, slow load times, outdated content, or a security vulnerability that's been quietly collecting spam form submissions.

Specifically, websites need ongoing attention for:

**Security updates.** Platforms like WordPress are powerful and flexible, but they require regular updates to the core software, themes, and plugins. Unpatched vulnerabilities are the most common source of hacked websites.

**Platform and plugin updates.** Updates are released regularly. Some are minor; some change functionality. Without someone managing these, things break quietly over time.

**Backups.** Regular off-site backups mean that if something goes catastrophically wrong, your site can be restored. Without backups, a hacked or crashed site can mean starting from scratch.

**Performance monitoring.** Page speed degrades over time as images accumulate, databases grow, and plugins add overhead. Regular optimization keeps load times fast.

**Content updates.** Services change. Prices change. Staff changes. A website that reflects outdated information damages credibility and may actively mislead customers.

**Broken link checking.** Links to other pages and external sites break over time. Broken links hurt user experience and SEO.

### What Monthly Maintenance Packages Typically Include

A standard small business website maintenance package from a professional agency typically includes:

- Monthly platform and plugin updates
- Regular security scans and patches
- Weekly or daily automated backups
- Uptime monitoring (alerts if your site goes down)
- Basic content updates (swapping text, updating images, adding announcements)
- Monthly performance report
- A set number of support hours for minor requests

In Canada, expect to pay **$75–$200/month** for a basic maintenance package from a boutique agency, depending on the platform and scope.

### What's Usually Not Included in Maintenance

Significant new features, new page designs, major content overhauls, or SEO campaigns typically fall outside a maintenance retainer and would be quoted separately.

### Do You Need a Maintenance Plan?

Ask yourself:

- **How technical are you?** If you're comfortable logging into a dashboard, running updates, and troubleshooting minor issues, you may be able to handle basic maintenance yourself. If technology isn't your strength, outsourcing this is worth the peace of mind.
- **How much does your website matter to your business?** If your site generates meaningful leads or revenue, downtime or a security breach has direct financial consequences. Protection is worth the monthly cost.
- **Do you have time?** Even if you're technically capable, is maintaining your website the best use of your time? For most business owners, the answer is no.

### Alternatives to a Full Maintenance Plan

If a monthly plan isn't right for you, some agencies offer:

- **Ad hoc support:** Pay by the hour when something comes up. No retainer, but higher per-hour rate.
- **Annual care plans:** Pay once per year for a set number of support hours and scheduled maintenance tasks.
- **DIY with training:** Have your designer walk you through the basics and handle it yourself, with the option to call in help for anything complex.
