## What Should a Professional Website Cost? (Canada Pricing Guide)

If you've been getting quotes for a professional website and wondering whether the numbers you're seeing are reasonable, you're not alone. Web design is one of those industries where pricing is notoriously opaque, and it can be genuinely hard to know if you're being quoted fairly.

Here's a clear, honest breakdown of what professional websites cost in Canada in 2024.

### The Short Answer

For most Canadian small businesses, a professionally designed, fully functional website with 5–8 pages should cost between **$1,000 and $3,500**.

If you're being quoted significantly under $1,000, ask very specific questions about what's included (and what isn't). If you're being quoted over $5,000 for a basic small business site with no e-commerce, booking systems, or complex custom features, you're likely being overcharged.

### The Full Price Spectrum

**DIY (Wix, Squarespace, Shopify):** $20–$50/month  
You do the work. Results depend entirely on your design skills and the time you invest. Suitable for testing a concept or for very simple needs.

**Junior Freelancer / New Designer:** $500–$1,200  
Limited experience but sometimes strong raw talent. Higher risk of delays, scope issues, or needing revisions later. Better for simple, low-stakes projects.

**Experienced Freelancer:** $1,000–$3,000  
A competent independent designer with a solid portfolio. Good option if you find someone with relevant experience and clear communication. Risk: no team behind them if something goes wrong.

**Boutique Agency (like ClickAdMedia):** $997–$5,000  
A dedicated team with a defined process, portfolio, and accountability. Best value-to-quality ratio for most small businesses. The price reflects not just design skills but strategy, process, and post-launch support.

**Mid-Sized Digital Agency:** $3,000–$15,000  
Larger teams, broader services, longer timelines. Worthwhile if you need a complex site or integrated digital marketing. Overkill for a standard small business site.

**Enterprise Agency:** $15,000–$100,000+  
Custom development, large teams, multi-month projects. For businesses with complex platforms, significant traffic, or highly specialized requirements.

### What Affects the Price

**Number of pages.** A 5-page site costs less than a 15-page site. Simple.

**Custom design vs. template-based.** A fully custom design takes more time and costs more. A well-customized template can look great and cost less — as long as it's done properly and not just swapped colours on a stock layout.

**Copy and content.** If your designer writes the content for your site, that adds to the price. If you provide the content, you save. Most agencies offer both options.

**E-commerce.** Adding a product catalogue, shopping cart, and payment processing significantly increases scope and cost.

**Integrations.** Booking systems, CRMs, email marketing platforms, live chat — each integration adds time and complexity.

**SEO scope.** Basic on-page SEO is standard. A full keyword research and content strategy engagement is a separate service.

**Photography.** Custom photography is usually an additional cost. Stock photography may be included or may require licensing.

### The Hidden Costs to Budget For

Beyond the build itself, account for:

- **Domain name:** $15–$30/year
- **Hosting:** $10–$30/month (sometimes bundled by agencies)
- **Professional email:** $6–$15/month per inbox
- **Ongoing maintenance:** $50–$200/month if you use an agency care plan
- **Future updates and changes:** Either your time or hourly charges

### Is Cheaper Always Worse?

Not always — but it often is. The question isn't "is this price low?" but "why is this price low?" A designer who charges less because they're efficient and have a streamlined process is fine. A designer who charges less because they cut corners, skip SEO, or use recycled templates is a false economy.

When something goes wrong with a cheap website — and it usually does eventually — the cost to fix it almost always exceeds the original "savings."
